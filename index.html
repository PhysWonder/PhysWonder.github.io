Abstract

Leave the abstract visible above the fold

The physical world is governed by material-dependent constitutive behaviors, yet current video generation models still struggle to produce physically plausible and controllable dynamics across diverse materials. We present PhysWonder, a unified framework built around simulation-coupled constitutive representations. PhysWonder-3D enables unified constitutive simulation and scalable physically grounded data synthesis through a decomposition-based hybrid MPM, while PhysWonder-Ctrl internalizes deformation-gradient-based constitutive control into a dual-branch video generation framework. By treating deformation gradients as simulation-coupled intermediate supervision, the model learns constitutive-aware latent control without requiring explicit structured control inputs during inference.

Stage 2

PhysWonder-Ctrl

PhysWonder-Ctrl internalizes deformation-gradient-based constitutive control into a dual-branch generation framework. Instead of requiring explicit control videos at inference time, it predicts constitutive-aware latent control from the learned internal representation.

The central message is that deformation gradients are a stronger intermediate signal than trajectories or sparse control cues, because they encode internal material response directly.
